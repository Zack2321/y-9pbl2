CyberAware

Your guide to online safety and security in the digital age

Why Online Safety Matters

The internet offers incredible opportunities for learning, communication, and entertainment. However, it also presents various risks including identity theft, malware, phishing scams, and privacy violations. Being aware of these dangers and knowing how to protect yourself is essential for everyone who goes online.

Use this resource to educate yourself and others about online risks and how to stay safe in the digital world.

Password Security

+

Strong passwords are your first line of defense against cyber threats. Follow these guidelines:

  • Use at least 12 characters with a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • Avoid using personal information like names or birthdates
  • Never reuse passwords across different accounts
  • Consider using a reputable password manager
  • Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible

Phishing Awareness

+

Phishing attacks try to trick you into revealing sensitive information. Recognize and avoid them:

  • Be suspicious of unsolicited emails asking for personal information
  • Check URLs before clicking - hover over links to see the actual address
  • Look for poor grammar and spelling - common in phishing attempts
  • Verify requests for sensitive information by contacting the company directly
  • Be cautious of urgent or threatening language designed to make you act quickly

Privacy Protection

+

Protecting your privacy online is crucial in the digital age:

  • Review and adjust privacy settings on social media platforms regularly
  • Be cautious about what personal information you share online
  • Use a VPN when connecting to public Wi-Fi networks
  • Regularly check app permissions on your devices
  • Consider using privacy-focused browsers and search engines

Malware Prevention

+

Protect your devices from malicious software:

  • Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware software
  • Keep your operating system and applications updated
  • Be cautious when downloading files or software from the internet
  • Don't open email attachments from unknown senders
  • Regularly back up your important data

Child Online Safety

+

Keep children safe in the digital world:

  • Educate children about online risks in an age-appropriate way
  • Set clear rules and boundaries for internet use
  • Use parental control software to filter and monitor activity
  • Keep devices in common areas where you can supervise usage
  • Encourage open communication about their online experiences
